NORTH GEORGIA TECHNICAL COLLEGE

WORK ETHICS POLICY

The Department of Technical and Adult Education has established a statewide work ethics policy with the assistance of business and industry in the state of Georgia.  Each technical institute/college will be utilizing this policy.  The policy is being marketed to business and industry to request transcripts from graduates to check the work ethics grades.  Work Ethics is a major issue with business and industry; therefore, it is very important to us.

There are ten work ethics traits and performance standards that will be taught to you.  As a student and worker, you are expected to exhibit these in the classrooms and laboratories.  On each of the ten traits, students will begin with a grade of 2, which means students have met expectations.  Meeting expectations means that you do what is expected of you.  For someone to receive a grade of 3 (exceptional), the student has to go above and beyond what is expected.  When a student does not exemplify what is expected, a point will be deducted from one or more of the traits.  This means that you need improvement.  At this point, an exception form will be completed with an improvement plan given to the student.  If the student does not accomplish the tasks of the plan, the student will not be able to regain the point back to the overall grade of “2”.  Also, more points may be deducted. 

The final Work Ethics grade will be determined by averaging all of the class Work Ethics grades for one student.  Grades will be assigned based on the following scale:

Work Ethics Grading Scale:

Grade Point Scale Description
3 24 - 30 Exceeds Expectations
2 20 - 23 Meets Expectations
1 17 - 19 Needs Improvement
0 0 - 16 Unacceptable

The Work Ethics Traits are as follows:

1.      Attendance

Attend 100 percent of the required class time
Be on time for each class and remain until the class ends
Be prepared for class by reading assignments and completing homework
Participate in activities by contributing to class discussion, completing assignments, and being involved in lab activities
Begin and end work as expected
Use work time appropriately
Notify instructor before planned absences or tardiness

 

2.      Character

Display a high level of effort and commitment to performing & completing work
Be honest in all situations
Demonstrate trustworthiness and responsible behavior
Adhere to the NGT Acceptable Use Policy for computer use

 

3.      Teamwork

Encourage & facilitate cooperation, pride, trust, and group identity
Foster commitment and team spirit
Facilitate cooperation

 

4.      Appearance

Present a neat, clean appearance
Practice personal hygiene
Wear clothing suitable to the job, task and environment
Wear full regulation uniform, if required

 

5.      Attitude

Display a willingness to cooperate and accept constructive criticism
Set realistic expectations

 

6.      Productivity

Observe established policies on safety
Notify proper authorities of circumstances or situations presenting potential safety hazards
Maintain equipment and supplies
Do not use or knowingly permit others to use tools & equipment improperly
Make up missed assignments in a timely manner
Stay on task and utilize time constructively

 

7.      Organizational Skills

Prioritize and manage time effectively
Demonstrate flexibility in adapting to changes

 

8.      Communication

Communicate accurate information to others in a professional & courteous manner
Demonstrate appropriate nonverbal communication skills
Listen attentively to others

 

9.      Cooperation

Convey a willingness to assist others
Work to resolve conflicts and to identify solutions in which all parties benefit
Demonstrate concern for treating people fairly and equitably
Follow the chain of command in resolving conflicts

 

10.      Respect

Treat instructors, staff, and fellow students with respect, courtesy, and tact
Do not engage in harassment of any kind
